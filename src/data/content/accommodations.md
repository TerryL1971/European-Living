# Accommodation Assistance

Finding the right place to stay in Germany can make or break your experience. Here's everything you need to know about hotels, hostels, apartments, and long-term housing.

---

## 🏨 Short-Term Stays (1–30 Days)

### Hotels

**Booking Platforms**
- [Booking.com](https://www.booking.com) – Most popular, easy cancellation
- [Hotels.com](https://www.hotels.com) – Rewards program (free night after 10 stays)
- [HRS.de](https://www.hrs.de) – German favorite, local pricing
- [Trivago](https://www.trivago.com) / [Kayak](https://www.kayak.com) – Compare prices across platforms

**Types of Hotels**
- Business Hotels (Ibis, Motel One, NH) – €60–100/night  
- Mid-Range (Mercure, Maritim) – €100–150/night  
- Boutique/Luxury – €150+/night

**Money-Saving Tips**
- Book directly after checking comparison sites  
- Stay outside the city center and use public transport  
- Always choose refundable rates  
- Search hotels near **S-Bahn** stations for easy access

---

### Hostels

Perfect for budget travelers and meeting people.

**Best Chains**
- A&O Hostels – Modern and clean  
- MEININGER Hotels – Hybrid hotel/hostel  
- Wombat’s – Great for social travelers

**Prices**
- Dorm bed: €15–30/night  
- Private room: €50–80/night

**Tips**
- Bring earplugs & a padlock  
- Book female-only dorms if preferred  
- Use [HostelWorld](https://www.hostelworld.com) or [Booking.com](https://www.booking.com)

---

### Airbnb & Vacation Rentals

**Pros**
- More space & privacy  
- Kitchen to save on meals  
- Great for groups

**Cons**
- Cleaning fees  
- Less flexible cancellation  
- Quality varies

**Alternatives**
- [FeWo-direkt](https://www.fewo-direkt.de)  
- [Vrbo](https://www.vrbo.com)  
- [9flats](https://www.9flats.com)

> ⚠️ **Note:** In Germany, many cities regulate Airbnb — make sure your host has permits!

## 🏢 Medium-Term Stays (1–6 Months)

### Serviced Apartments

Fully furnished with hotel-like services.

**Recommended Platforms**
- [Homelike](https://www.thehomelike.com)  
- [Wunderflats](https://www.wunderflats.com)  
- [Urban Ground](https://www.urbanground.de)

**Average Costs**
- Studio: €800–1,500/month  
- 1-bed: €1,000–2,000/month  

---

### Subletting (Zwischenmiete)

**Where to Find**
- [WG-Gesucht.de](https://www.wg-gesucht.de)  
- Facebook Groups ("Expats in [City]")  
- [Immobilienscout24.de](https://www.immobilienscout24.de)

**Red Flags**
- Landlord refuses video call  
- Asks for money upfront  
- Too-good-to-be-true prices

## 🏡 Long-Term Housing (6+ Months)

### Finding an Apartment

**Top Sites**
- [Immobilienscout24.de](https://www.immobilienscout24.de)  
- [Immonet.de](https://www.immonet.de)  
- [WG-Gesucht.de](https://www.wg-gesucht.de)

**Key Terms**
| German | Meaning |
| ------- | -------- |
| **Kaltmiete** | Rent excluding utilities |
| **Warmmiete** | Rent including utilities |
| **Nebenkosten** | Utility costs |
| **Kaution** | Deposit (2–3 months rent) |

---

### The Application Process

You’ll typically need:
- Schufa (credit report)  
- Proof of income  
- Copy of ID/passport  
- Mieterselbstauskunft form

> 💡 **Tip:** Bring your full application printed and ready — competition can be fierce.

---

### Registration (Anmeldung)

You **must register** your address within 14 days.

**Bring:**
- Anmeldung form  
- Wohnungsgeberbestätigung (landlord form)  
- Passport  
- Rental contract

## 💶 Cost Overview

| City | Studio | 1-Bed | WG Room |
|------|---------|-------|---------|
| Berlin | €700–1,000 | €900–1,400 | €400–700 |
| Munich | €1,000–1,500 | €1,200–1,800 | €600–900 |
| Frankfurt/Hamburg | €800–1,200 | €1,000–1,500 | €500–800 |

**Extras**
- Utilities: €100–200/month  
- Internet: €20–40/month  
- GEZ Tax: €18.36/month  

---

## 🚫 Scam Warnings

**Avoid listings that:**
- Demand wire transfers  
- Don’t allow viewings  
- Use vague or broken English  
- Offer prices way below market

---

## 🗣️ Useful German Phrases

- *Ich suche eine Wohnung* – I’m looking for an apartment  
- *Sind Haustiere erlaubt?* – Are pets allowed?  
- *Welche Nebenkosten kommen dazu?* – What are additional costs?
